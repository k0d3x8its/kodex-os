# 🏗️ List of Materials

Here is the list of requirements to conduct this system. The only thing on this list that is truly necessary is the memo book and pen. Everything else is just extra.

---

## Field Notes & Moleskine

These do not need to be the Field Notes brand but merely a pocket notebook — hereafter referred to as **memo book** — or two that you can keep on you. I happen to use Field Notes & Moleskine.

### Field Notes

The Field Notes memo books are the core of this system. They are used for everything except for reference books and extensive research of any kind. Any memo book could be used.

#### Memo Books

These are used for documenting:
- Field logs
- Field Research
- Field Study
- Work Hours

> [Field Notes Memo Books](https://fieldnotesbrand.com)

#### Notebooks

These are used to merely extend research that starts within a memo book.

> [Field Notes Pitch Black Notebook](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/products/pitch-black-notebook)

**Sub-pages:**
- **Field Logs** — For logging daily entries
- **Field Research** — For conducting research
- **Field Study** — For deeper research worth preserving
- **Work Hours** — For tracking work hours

### Moleskine

Moleskine notebooks are very nice and reliable. I use the Cahier pocket notebooks to create reference books I can use when needed.

> Used for: **References** — Physical reference guides compiled from research, stored in Moleskine Cahier pocket journals.

## ✒️ Pen & Refills

Any pen will work; it is really all about which pen suits you. I went through a couple of pens before landing on the ones below.

### Field Notes Engraved Space Pen

I chose this pen because I like the idea of having a pen in my pocket. I prefer the Big i Design slim click pen because I can change ink refills to what I want.

### Big i Design — Slim Click Pen

I like being able to try different refills and keep the same design in my hand. This allows me to look for the ink I like rather than the feeling of a pen and the ink I like. This is my primary EDC pen. It feels good when writing even though it is a little heavier than your average pen. The weight is an adjustment but it is not something I worry about.

---

## 🔗 Short Links

These are used to reference things I have researched or plan to research. They can really be used for any reason to reference something on the web.

### Bit.ly

I use [Bit.ly](https://bit.ly) because it is a short URL and has an app. I tried finding a domain shorter than 'bit' but I could not find one.

### Anon.to

[Anon.to](https://anon.to) is an anonymous open source short link generator that creates an anonymous short link which also hides referrers. I do not use this one very often anymore considering I prefer to change the domain name to my liking. This does not allow you to change the characters within the link.

---

## 📦 Archival Box

This is basically a storage container of some kind to house your archived memo books. I use a box made by Field Notes Brand to organize Field Notes memo books. It comes with card dividers to organize within the box. [The Field Notes Archive envelopes](https://www.instructables.com/Field-Notes-Archival-Envelopes/) fit perfectly in these boxes.

### Archival Wooden Box

This was chosen because it is a complement from the company that makes the memo books I purchase — Field Notes. It is intended to archive memo books and I intend to archive.

---

## 📜 Cardstock & Adhesive *(Optional)*

The cardstock can be any color you want and any weight you want. Only requirement is 8.5" by 11" card stock. The weight I use is 100# stock. Adhesive I use is roll-on scotch tape. You will need something to cut the cardstock with. I use an X-acto knife.

### Scotch Tape Runner Extra Strength

I use this because the last type I used did not hold the archive envelopes together for very long. I would prefer to find something stronger and with similar convenience.

---

## 📷 Canon Ivy *(Optional)*

I use this for adding images to my research or my daily logs. Originally I would provide a link to an image. Now I provide a sticker picture when relevant. The sticker picture helps visualize in the moment rather than typing a link into a web browser.

---

## 📱 Scannable App *(Optional)*

This is an Evernote app that scans documents automatically. I'm sure a better one exists but I have been using this since Evernote released it.

---

## 🗒️ Sticky Notes *(Optional)*

I use sticky notes to add and hide notes in my research memo book. Sometimes it is relevant, for me, to add a sticky note with information and then flip it to reveal more information that may be contrasting, correlated, etc.

I use 10 standard 3in × 3in sticky notes posted in the back of the research memo book cover. 10 sticky notes usually lasts me the life of the memo book.
