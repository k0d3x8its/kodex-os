# 🗃️ Phase IV — Archiving

The Archiving phase is about, you guessed it, archiving. This phase explains why something gets archived and when is it worthy of archiving. It consists of the things I am done with and organizing them accordingly. This is a simple procedure to follow.

---

## Epoch I — Archive

Once I have decided to archive a memo book I start this Epoch. I gather an envelope and fill out all information on the cover that is relevant to the memo book being archived. This will require providing pertinent information of the memo book to be documented on the cover of the envelope. This will help when glancing at the covers to find what you are looking for. After documenting the information on the cover of the envelope, then you can move forward with the next epoch — Organize.

If you plan to take an extra step and digitally archive the pages in your memo book, do it at the end of your day — but only if you documented something. Scan the pages and process them into the **LLM Wiki** (Obsidian). Each memo book maps to a dedicated folder or section in the LLM Wiki, giving you a true 1:1 relationship between physical books and digital entries. The LLM is used to extract, structure, and tag the raw content from the scan into organized wiki notes. When a memo book is used up, its digital counterpart in the LLM Wiki should already be complete and linked to its archive record.

---

## Epoch II — Organize

This Epoch is merely keeping all archived memo books organized. I organize these envelopes with a numerical catalog. On the back of the envelope is a section to provide a number to organize the envelopes. This can really be done in any fashion. I use the Field Notes Archival Box to archive and organize my memo books.

Each category gets a different color envelope:
- **Daily Logs** = kraft brown
- **Research** = gray
- **Work Hours** = white
