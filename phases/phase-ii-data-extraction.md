# 🗂️ Phase II — Data Extraction

The data extraction phase happens after things have been documented. Once documented, extraction is needed to begin further research. One example would be conducting extensive research on something that has been documented. This requires transferring all data/information from your research memo book into a [Field Notes notebook](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/products/pitch-black-notebook) dedicated to that subject alone. This saves space in your day-to-day memo book.

If you are using the **LLM Wiki** as part of your stack, this phase is also the natural point to open a dedicated Wiki entry for the subject being extracted — running digitally in parallel with the physical notebook. If you are not using the LLM Wiki, this phase operates entirely in the physical layer, Layer 0 of the Kodex Stack, and nothing changes.

---

## Epoch I — Review

> ⚠️ Things must be documented before this Epoch can begin. This Epoch can be conducted at any time after documentation.

Once something has been documented it is up for review at any time. The review of information collected is necessary to extract, analyze, and process. The Review Epoch is about making a determination on the information being valuable enough to dive in further — otherwise it stays in the memo book and does not move to the next epoch for extraction.

---

## Epoch II — Extraction

> 💡 The extraction epoch is all predicated on what you want to learn more about from the things you have documented in the previous Document Epoch. This is more of a personal preference guided by your interests in the moment.

Once you have decided that you want to learn more about something that you have documented in the previous phase, open a [Field Notes notebook](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/products/pitch-black-notebook) and start documenting more extensively on that subject or interest. This will become something of a study guide for this subject later on. At this point it may be relevant to create a physical reference guide for the subject or interest. Reference guides go into [Moleskine Cahier pocket journals](https://www.moleskine.com/en-us/shop/notebooks/journals/cahier-journals/).

> **LLM Wiki (optional):** If you are using the LLM Wiki, this is where a dedicated Wiki entry for the subject would be opened and linked to the physical notebook — the two running in parallel. The physical extraction process is the same regardless.
