# 🔬 Phase III — Analysis & Processing

This phase starts after everything has been extracted for a particular subject or research. This is where you analyze what you have and explore further if needed. You then process what you have gathered and determine if you need more. Am I content with what I have, or is it ready to be archived?

> 💡 For those using the **LLM Wiki**, this phase also includes analyzing and refining the corresponding Wiki entry — confirming that the extracted notes are accurate, tagged correctly, and cross-linked as needed. The core question, however, is the same regardless of your stack: *Am I done? Is it ready to archive?*

---

## Epoch I — Analyze

This Epoch is where you analyze what you have extracted to a Field Study or kept in the Field Research. Either way, this is where you absorb the information you have notated and analyze it. Analyzation is about determining what to keep and what to leave behind. What is relevant? What is not?

> 💡 **LLM Wiki (optional):** If you are using the LLM Wiki, this is where you analyze the Wiki entry for this subject — correcting, expanding, or trimming what the extraction produced. Run /kos-lint as well. The physical analysis process is the same regardless.

---

## Epoch II — Process

Once you have analyzed the information you have documented and extracted, you process it. Processing is a determination of where the information goes. Will it stay on your person, or will you archive it?

This is evaluated by the relevancy of the information:
- Is it worthy enough to stay in my pocket?
- Should I archive it?
- Am I done with it?

> 💡 **LLM Wiki (optional):** If you are using the LLM Wiki, this is more automated and complex. Essentially, processing means marking the corresponding Wiki entry as complete and ready to be linked to the archive record. This closes the loop between the physical book and its digital counterpart.
